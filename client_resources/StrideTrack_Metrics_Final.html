StrideTrack Metrics Reference

Priority Legend: 🟢 MVP | 🟡 Nice to Have

UNIVERSAL METRICS (Applied to All Events)

These metrics are calculated and displayed for every event type. Event-specific sections below only contain metrics unique to that discipline.

Core Temporal Metrics

Metric Formula Priority Visualization Explanation
Ground Contact Time (GCT) Direct measurement (L/R) 🟢 MVP Step-by-step line chart (L/R overlay) Time the foot spends on the ground. Shorter GCT at high speeds = better reactive strength. Elite sprinters: < 100ms at max velocity.
Flight Time (FT) Direct measurement (L/R) 🟢 MVP Step-by-step line chart (L/R overlay) Time spent in the air between contacts. Reflects vertical impulse generated. Longer FT at same speed = more power.
Step Time GCT + FT 🟢 MVP Stacked bar (GCT + FT per step) Total duration of one step. Inverse of step frequency. Consistent values indicate rhythm stability.
Stride Time Step_Time_L + Step_Time_R 🟡 Nice Line chart over strides Duration of a full stride (left + right step). Used for analyzing overall rhythm and bilateral differences.

Counting Metrics

Metric Formula Priority Visualization Explanation
Total Steps Count of ground contacts 🟢 MVP Single KPI card Basic volume metric.
Mean GCT Σ(GCT) / n 🟢 MVP KPI card + horizontal reference line on charts Average ground contact time. Summary metric for overall efficiency. Lower is generally better at max velocity.
Mean Flight Time Σ(FT) / n 🟢 MVP KPI card + horizontal reference line on charts Average flight time. Summary metric for overall power output. Context-dependent (sprinting vs jumping).

Ratio Metrics

Metric Formula Priority Visualization Explanation
Reactive Strength Index (RSI) FT / GCT 🟢 MVP Line chart over steps + KPI card for mean Key indicator of leg spring stiffness and reactive ability. Higher RSI = more “bounce.” Elite sprinters: RSI > 1.0 at max velocity.
Duty Factor GCT / Step_Time 🟡 Nice Line chart over steps Proportion of step time on ground. < 0.5 = flight-dominant (running). > 0.5 = ground-dominant (walking/fatigue).
Contact-Flight Index (FT − GCT) / Step_Time 🟡 Nice Line chart over steps Balance between flight and contact. Positive = flight-dominant, negative = contact-dominant.

Asymmetry Metrics

Metric Formula Priority Visualization Explanation
GCT Asymmetry % |GCT_L − GCT_R| / ((GCT_L + GCT_R) / 2) × 100 🟢 MVP Gauge (0-15% range) or KPI with color threshold (customizable) Percentage difference in GCT between feet. > 10% may indicate injury risk or compensation. Target: < 5%.
FT Asymmetry % |FT_L − FT_R| / ((FT_L + FT_R) / 2) × 100 🟢 MVP Gauge or KPI Push-off power difference between legs. Often correlates with GCT asymmetry.
ΔGCT L-R GCT_L − GCT_R 🟡 Nice Diverging bar chart (centered at 0) Raw difference in ms. Positive = left longer, negative = right longer. Shows direction of imbalance.
ΔFT L-R FT_L − FT_R 🟡 Nice Diverging bar chart (centered at 0) Raw flight time difference. Shows direction and magnitude of push-off asymmetry.

Performance Metrics

Metric Formula Priority Visualization Explanation
Step Frequency 1 / Step_Time (Hz) 🟢 MVP Line chart over steps + KPI for mean Steps per second. Key sprint variable. Elite sprinters: 4.5-5.0 Hz at max velocity.
N. Steps in GCT Range value1 < count(steps_GCT) < value2 🟢 MVP Pie chart (ranges customizable by user) Count of steps meeting target criteria (e.g., “how many steps under 100ms, over 200ms and in the middle?”).

Session-Level Metrics

Metric Formula Priority Visualization Explanation
Mean RSI Σ(RSI) / n 🟢 MVP KPI card Average RSI across all steps. Summary of overall reactive ability for the session.

Hurdle-Specific Metrics

Metric Formula Priority Visualization Explanation
Hurdle Split Time between consecutive hurdle clearances 🟢 MVP Bar chart (split per hurdle) + KPI for consistency Primary performance metric. Consistent splits = rhythm mastery. Elite 110mH: ~1.0s splits.
Steps Between Hurdles Count of ground contacts between hurdles 🟢 MVP Integer display per interval (ideal = 3 for 110mH) Should be constant (3 for 110mH, 7 for 400mH). Changes indicate technical breakdown.
Take-off GCT GCT of last step before hurdle 🟢 MVP One bar for each hurdle Quick take-off (< 100ms) enables aggressive attack. Increasing = fatigue.
Landing GCT GCT of first step after hurdle 🟢 MVP One bar for each hurdle Always longer than normal (impact absorption). Should stay < 130ms.
Take-off FT FT during hurdle clearance 🟢 MVP One bar for each hurdle Hurdle flight time. Should be efficient, not excessive (~300-350ms).
GCT Increase Hurdle-to-Hurdle (GCT_hurdle_N − GCT_hurdle_1) / GCT_hurdle_1 × 100 🟡 Nice KPI card Hurdle-specific fatigue. > 15% increase signals conditioning issues.

BOSCO TEST (Repeated Jumps)

Universal Metrics for GCT, FT, and RSI are included. Below are Bosco-specific metrics.

Jump Height Metrics

Metric Formula Priority Visualization Explanation
Jump Height h = (g × FT²) / 8 = 1.226 × FT² (m, with FT in seconds) 🟢 MVP Bar chart per jump with mean line and peak highlighted Primary outcome. Calculated from flight time using physics formula.
Mean Jump Height Σ(h) / n 🟢 MVP KPI card + horizontal reference line Overall jumping ability for the session.
Peak Jump Height max(h) 🟢 MVP KPI card (highlighted max bar) Best single effort. Gap between peak and mean indicates consistency.

Rhythm & Power Metrics

Metric Formula Priority Visualization Explanation
Jump Frequency n_jumps / total_time (Hz) 🟢 MVP KPI card Reflects rhythm and reactive ability during continuous jumping.
Contact Time Efficiency h / GCT 🟡 Nice Line chart over jumps Height per ms of ground contact. Higher = more efficient.
Specific Power (W/kg) (g² × FT × (FT + GCT)) / (4 × GCT) 🟡 Nice Line chart + KPI for mean Power normalized to body mass. Allows comparison across athletes.
Specific Energy (J/kg) g × h 🟡 Nice Bar chart per jump Work done per kg of body mass.
Total Power (W) Specific_Power × body_mass 🟡 Nice KPI card Absolute power output (requires body mass input).
Total Energy (J) Specific_Energy × body_mass 🟡 Nice KPI card Absolute energy per jump (requires body mass input).

Bosco Fatigue Metrics

Metric Formula Priority Visualization Explanation
Fatigue Index ((h_initial − h_final) / h_initial) × 100 🟢 MVP KPI gauge (0-30% typical range) Key Bosco outcome. Shows performance maintenance. < 15% is good.
RSI Decay (RSI_first_5 − RSI_last_5) / RSI_first_5 × 100 🟡 Nice Before/after comparison bar More sensitive fatigue indicator than height alone.

Visualization Description

Fatigue Analysis Split view: first 2 jumps vs last 2 jumps comparison.

REACTION TIME TEST

Standalone test — does not use Universal Metrics.

Metric Formula Priority Visualization Explanation
Reaction Time Time from stimulus to first movement (detected via GCT onset) 🟢 MVP KPI card with color zones (<200ms green, 200-300ms yellow, >300ms red) — customizable Pure neural response speed. Elite sprinters: < 150ms. False start: < 100ms.
Movement Time Time from first movement till foot touches ground 🟡 Nice KPI card Motor execution speed after reaction.
Total Response Time Reaction_Time + Movement_Time 🟡 Nice Stacked bar (RT + MT segments) Complete response from stimulus to action completion.

RSI Reference Values (Context-Dependent)

Context 🟢 Good 🟡 Average 🔴 Low
Sprinting (max velocity) >1.1 0.9-1.1 <0.9
Hurdles (between hurdles) >1.0 0.8-1.0 <0.8
Jumps (approach) >1.2 1.0-1.2 <1.0
Bosco Test (continuous) >1.0 0.7-1.0 <0.7

Notes

  • GCT = Ground Contact Time (milliseconds)
  • FT = Flight Time (milliseconds)
  • RSI = Reactive Strength Index (higher = more reactive/elastic)
  • g = 9.81 m/s² (gravitational acceleration)
  • All asymmetry metrics use absolute value to capture magnitude regardless of direction
  • Threshold values for color coding (Asymmetry %, Drift %, Reaction Time) are customizable in user settings
