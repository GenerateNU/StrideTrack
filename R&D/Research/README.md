# Reasearch Directory
I took data from two seperate studies conducted with different coaches.

Study 1:

Brewer, Mollie, et al. “Coach, Data Analyst, and Protector: Exploring Data Practices of Collegiate Coaching Staff.” Proceedings of the 2025 CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems, 25 Apr. 2025, pp. 1–13, https://doi.org/10.1145/3706598.3714026.

‌Used 17 collegiate coaching staff for 3 womens and 3 mends teams at a US university.
Participants included:
    1) Coaches
    2) Dietitians
    3) Athletic Trainers
    4) Sports Scientist
    5) Administractive staff
What was found:
Coaching staff gathered data through different phases of the athletes season including Pre season, competition phase, and recovery.
They establish baselines for their athletes in the preseason assessments. They do this in order to assess current fitness levels and what to work on/ train for the upcoming season.
As the season begins they find daily monitoring is key, to understand the correlation of competition performance and intensity of practices.
General issues encountered with interfaces:
Older coaches typically want a simple interface so they dont have to spend time learning it or really spend as little time as possible understanding it.
Student athletes get overwhelmed and internalize when there is too much data thrown at them at once.
Generally speaking they want a streamlined approach. Something simple and easy to understand.
They want something that will support rapid decision making and actionable insights.
Most currently share data using a spreadsheet they share with others.
Flagging changes in athlete performace to be able to check in with the athletes as soon as possible.
They look for difference between in-game performances and practice performance metrics.
Long term trends as well and see historical data.
They also have a common frustration with black-box metrics, unable to see formulas used. They want to know the formulas we are using to calculate the metrics.
Coaches want to manage the flow of data in ways that will protect the emotional well being of their athletes.
Filters wanted to be able to see selective data quickly, they specifically dont want to overwhelm students with large datasets to prevent athletes overthinking and getting in their heads.
Using a simple color-coded system such as green yellow and red was brought up as well.
Using a point system to gamify data, adding incentives for student athletes to preform well, not required but I was thinking of adding medals in an attempt to help to modivate the athletes espeically when their reach certain goals or beat personal bests.
Use line charts maybe, to show longitudinal views of data and historical trends.
Transparency was the main theme wanted by the coaches.
Systems should provide access to raw data to allow for spealized use cases.


Study 2:

Rauff, Erica L, et al. “Using Sport Science Data in Collegiate Athletics: Coaches’ Perspectives.” International Journal of Sports Science & Coaching, vol. 17, no. 3, 21 Jan. 2022, p. 174795412110651, https://doi.org/10.1177/17479541211065146.

This study interviewed 58 coaches in the UK: Sports coaches represented in the study included...
Baseball, softball, women's rowing, women's volleyball, and men's and women's teams for the following sports:
    Basketball, soccer, cross country, track and field, golf, and swimming.
Head strength and conditioning coach for all teams also participated.
Coaches have a mean age of 40 years. 77% were male and 84% were caucasian. The coaches had been coaching as well for a mean of 15.1 years.


Questions: Applicable added!

1) Can you think of specific ways that your team would benefit from knowledge gained from collecting data?
16.21% said: Difficult to have conversations if you are going off qualitative things.

16.21% said: Dont have a great way to compile progress over time and have a way to reflect back on it.

2) Are there specific performance questions you would like answered when it comes to your team?
14.70% said: Deficiencies with your gait as your running.
11.76% said: Data on how hard they are working.

3) Was not applicable to us.

4) If you were given an opportunity to to collect data throughout the season, what specific questions would you want to see supported by the data?
8.69% said: Overall performance (historical data!).

4b) How do you think your team would use the data provided during the season?
28.57% said: Training adjustments.
14.28% said: Let the athletes see firsthand “athlete buy-in”.
9.52% said: Habit of reporting, then they look at those numbers, “process evaluation”.

5A) Are there any concerns to collecting data on your teams?
15.55% said: Timing of tests in where you are in the season
11.11% said: Being able to present the information where they can become aware, where they dont feel threatened or embarrassed.

5b) Not applicable.
